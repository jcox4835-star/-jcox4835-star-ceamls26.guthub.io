problem: |
  Alzheimer’s Disease affects millions of people worldwide and is one of the leading causes of cognitive decline in older adults. As the disease progresses, patients experience memory loss, reduced problem-solving ability, and difficulty performing everyday tasks. Families, caregivers, and healthcare systems are also heavily impacted by the emotional and financial burden associated with long-term care. Although current treatments may temporarily reduce symptoms, there is still no widely effective cure that can stop or significantly slow disease progression.
One major challenge in Alzheimer’s research is the lengthy and expensive process of discovering new drug compounds. Traditional drug discovery methods require years of laboratory testing and clinical validation, which limits the speed at which new therapies can reach patients. This project addresses that gap by exploring how artificial intelligence and machine learning can accelerate the identification of promising drug candidate compounds that may help slow cognitive decline in Alzheimer’s patients. By combining biomedical datasets with AI-driven analysis, the project aims to support faster and more efficient therapeutic discovery.
approach: |
  Describe how you'll approach the problem. A bulleted plan works well:

  Step 1 — Collect and Prepare Data
Gather publicly available biomedical and chemical datasets related to Alzheimer’s disease, including gene expression data, protein interaction data, and known drug compound information from databases such as National Institutes of Health resources and Alzheimer’s research repositories. Clean and preprocess the datasets for machine learning analysis.
Step 2 — Build the AI Prediction Model
Develop a machine learning pipeline capable of identifying patterns between molecular compound properties and biological pathways associated with cognitive decline. Tools such as Python, TensorFlow, Scikit-learn, and molecular data analysis libraries will be used to train predictive models.
Step 3 — Evaluate Candidate Compounds
Test and rank predicted drug compounds based on factors such as biological relevance, predicted effectiveness, and safety indicators. Visualization methods and statistical analysis will be used to compare model performance and validate findings against existing Alzheimer’s research literature.
Step 4 — Present and Share Results
Compile findings into a research presentation, technical report, and poster summarizing the AI workflow, datasets used, and identified candidate compounds. Results will be communicated in a format accessible to both technical and non-technical audiences.

outcome: |
  By the end of the program, the project is expected to produce an AI-driven prototype system capable of analyzing biomedical data and identifying potential drug candidate compounds associated with slowing cognitive decline in Alzheimer’s disease. The project will also generate a ranked list of promising compounds for future experimental investigation and provide visualizations showing how the AI model reached its predictions.

Additional deliverables will include a research poster, a short technical paper, and presentation materials summarizing the methodology, datasets, and findings. These outputs will allow researchers, students, and mentors to better understand how artificial intelligence can support faster drug discovery efforts in neurodegenerative disease research and may serve as a foundation for future translational biomedical studies.
