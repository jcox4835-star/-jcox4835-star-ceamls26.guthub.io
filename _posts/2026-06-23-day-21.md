   Today we spent the entire day in our research lab focusing on completing the assignments that Ramisa assigned to us. My primary task involved working with the ASK1 dataset and identifying active and inactive classifications as well as generating pIC50 regression values. Throughout the day, I continued using Google Colab to process the dataset and work through the coding steps needed to prepare the data for analysis. A large portion of the work involved importing the correct subsets of data, checking for missing values, and making sure the information being used was valid for the machine learning models. Once the dataset was properly organized, I cleaned the data and prepared it for QSAR modeling. After completing the cleaning process, I ran a QSAR model using the cleaned dataset and analyzed the results. I also generated and interpreted a confusion matrix, which provided a visual representation of the model's performance through true positives, true negatives, false positives, and false negatives. In addition to the model evaluation, I created summary tables, active and inactive distribution tables, and label consistency reports to better understand the overall structure and quality of the dataset. These activities helped reinforce many of the machine learning and data analysis concepts we have been learning throughout the program. Overall, the day was highly focused on coding, data processing, and strengthening our understanding of how QSAR workflows are applied to real research datasets.
