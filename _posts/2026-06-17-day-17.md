what_i_learned: |
   Today we started the day in our research labs by continuing to work on data cleaning for our targeted proteins. My focus was on the amyloid-beta dataset, and I spent most of the morning identifying and addressing missing values to ensure the data was complete and reliable. In cases where values were missing, I used appropriate methods, such as replacing them with the mean when necessary, to maintain the integrity of the dataset. After cleaning the data, I added descriptors and notes to help explain what each feature represented so the dataset would be easier to understand and interpret later. Once I finished preparing the dataset, I saved the cleaned version and began using it for QSAR model development and practice. After lunch, I focused primarily on building and testing machine learning models using the cleaned dataset. I trained and evaluated three different models: Decision Tree, Random Forest Classifier, and Logistic Regression. Comparing the results from each model helped me better understand how different machine learning approaches perform when analyzing the same data. After completing the modeling process and reviewing the accuracy metrics, our group shifted our attention to preparing for the weekly showcase. Along with Ranjish, Austin, Destiny, and Ramisa, we worked on finalizing our presentation slides and making sure everything was organized and ready for presentation.
