what_i_learned: | 
   Today we started the day in our research lab by preparing for our Midway Symposium practice presentation. As a team, we continued reviewing our slides and making small adjustments to improve the overall flow of our presentation. My main responsibility was refining the literature review section, so I spent time reviewing the studies we analyzed, the papers we read, and making sure I could clearly explain how they contributed to our Alzheimer's disease research project. Rather than simply reading from the slides, I focused on understanding the material so I could present it naturally and confidently. Before lunch, our team completed a full practice run of the presentation, which helped us identify that we still needed to improve our timing, especially during the live demonstration portions. After lunch, we met once more to make final adjustments and practiced again before heading to another building for our mock symposium presentations. Our group was paired with Groups 1 and 8, giving us the opportunity to present our research and observe the work of other teams. It was interesting to see the different ways each group approached their research and presented their findings. The practice session also provided valuable feedback that will help us improve before the official Midway Symposium. Overall, today was focused on strengthening our presentation skills, improving our confidence, and making sure our team is fully prepared for next week's presentation.
