what_i_learned: | 
      Today our main focus was completing a molecular docking demo assignment to help us become familiar with the software and workflow we will be using throughout the next stage of our research. We worked with ChimeraX and AlphaFold 3 while following the docking process step by step so we could better understand how each tool contributes to drug discovery. Since my project focuses on the ASK1 protein, I first located the appropriate protein target before identifying three additional compounds that could potentially interact with it. After selecting the compounds, I collected important information for each one, including the PubChem CID and SMILES notation, which are essential for preparing molecular docking analyses. Once all of the required information was gathered, I entered the data into Protenix and used AlphaFold 3 to begin visualizing how the selected compounds could interact with my protein target. It was interesting to see the protein and compounds represented in three-dimensional structures because it made the molecular interactions much easier to understand than simply reading about them in research papers. This hands-on activity helped connect many of the concepts we have been studying over the past few weeks, including QSAR modeling, protein structures, and molecular docking. Throughout the assignment, I continued asking questions and comparing my work with my teammates to make sure I understood each step before moving on. During the last hour of the day, our group also began preparing slides for tomorrow's weekly showcase by organizing our recent progress and planning what each team member would present. Overall, today was a great introduction to molecular docking and gave me much more confidence as we prepare to begin applying these techniques to our actual research project.
