what_i_learned: |
  We started off the day in our research lab with Ramisa and Ranjish, where we discussed some of the research papers we had selected and reviewed. During the session, Ramisa assigned us additional papers to read so we could continue building a stronger understanding of QSAR and how it connects to our Alzheimer’s disease research project. We spent time talking through important concepts from the articles and preparing to dive deeper into databases and research tools moving forward. Before lunch, we attended a session with Dr. Anita Pandey, who spent nearly two hours teaching us about literature reviews and their importance in research programs like this one. She explained how literature reviews help researchers organize information, identify patterns, and better understand the direction of their work. Dr. Pandey also introduced us to visual abstracts and emphasized how visual communication can help make complex information easier to understand and present to others. After lunch, we participated in a very interactive activity called Amazon Careers, which combined video clips and Kahoot-style questions to teach us about the behind-the-scenes world of sports streaming. Through the activity, we learned about topics such as infrastructure, bandwidth, lagging, and the technology needed to support large streaming platforms like Amazon Prime. To end the day, we returned to our Python 101 classes where we continued learning about functions and practiced our coding skills by creating a Powerball number generator. Overall, the day was both educational and engaging because it combined research, communication, technology, and coding into one productive experience.
