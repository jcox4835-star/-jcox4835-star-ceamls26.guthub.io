what_i_learned: |
  Today marked the beginning of the in-person portion of the Summer Institute as everyone gathered in the Social Sciences Building. We started the day with introductions and an overview from Michael, who explained how the in-person portion of the program will operate and what we should expect over the coming weeks. After that, Elijah gave us a brief introduction to the Midway Symposium and discussed what will be expected of us as we prepare our presentations and research findings. We then attended a workshop with Dr. Dacon, who reviewed important concepts related to Google Colab, datasets, and confusion matrices, helping reinforce many of the machine learning topics we have been studying throughout the program. Following the workshop, we had lunch before attending a session led by an education professor who provided valuable insight into artificial intelligence and structural bias. The discussion encouraged us to think critically about how AI systems are developed and the potential challenges that can arise when working with data. Later in the afternoon, we participated in a team-building mixer activity where we worked together to build a simple marshmallow structure. The activity was both fun and collaborative, allowing participants to interact and get to know one another better. To finish the day, we walked over to our research lab and spent time discussing the next steps of our project while continuing to build stronger relationships within our group. Overall, it was a productive first day on campus that helped us become more familiar with both the program and the people we will be working alongside throughout the remainder of the Institute.
