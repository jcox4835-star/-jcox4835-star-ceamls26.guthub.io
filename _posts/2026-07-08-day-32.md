   Today I started the morning by making sure all of the necessary project files were uploaded and organized in our shared Google Drive. One of the main files I checked included our classification and regression model comparisons, and I made sure everything was uploaded correctly before confirming with Ramisa that all of my work was complete. After that, we continued our literature review assignments, and my paper focused on the role of cheminformatics in drug discovery for Alzheimer's disease. The paper explained how cheminformatics combines chemistry, biology, and computer science to analyze large collections of compounds and identify those with the greatest therapeutic potential. One of the most interesting topics discussed was the importance of the blood-brain barrier (BBB) and how it helps researchers determine whether potential drug compounds are capable of reaching the brain. Since Alzheimer's disease affects the brain, understanding which compounds can successfully cross the BBB is a critical step in identifying effective drug candidates. I shared the key findings from my paper with my teammates, and our discussion helped all of us gain a better understanding of how cheminformatics fits into the overall drug discovery pipeline. After lunch, we participated in SAIRI Connect, where we spent time building relationships with other students in the program through interactive team-building activities. We played a name game that challenged our communication and memory skills, and my teammate Kenzie and I finished in fourth place. To end the day, we watched the movie Mercy, starring Chris Pratt, which gave everyone an opportunity to relax and enjoy time together after another productive day of research. Overall, today provided a great balance between continuing our scientific learning and strengthening the connections within our cohort.
